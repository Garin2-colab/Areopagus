# Style Blueprint: David Carson

### 👁️ The Visual Signature
The king of "grunge typography" and the defining voice of *Ray Gun* magazine. Carson threw out the grid system entirely. His core philosophy is that "you cannot confuse legibility with communication"—meaning a messy, torn layout can communicate an emotion much better than a clean, readable one.

### 🎨 Color & Light
*   **Street-Level Chaos:** Colors feel pulled from urban environments—faded poster ink, street spray-paint, overlapping magazine clippings, and raw cardboard beige.
*   **Flattened Contrast:** Often relies on photocopying artifacts, where midtones are crushed and colors overlap haphazardly without pristine digital gradients.

### 📐 Composition & Texture
*   **The Anti-Grid:** Text overlaps text. Leading and kerning are completely broken. Layouts are treated like physical collages where elements crash into each other.
*   **Type as Image:** Letters are chopped, flipped upside down, or oversized, forcing the viewer to look at the *shape* of the word before they read its meaning.

> **Creative Prompt Generator Idea:** "Apply a David Carson lens: Break the alignment. Overlap two crucial text elements and let the textures of the background crop through the letterforms."
